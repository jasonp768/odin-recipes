Thanksgiving Stuffing

Description:

When Thanksgiving comes around, theres only one dish that I look forward to more than the turkey. That's the Thanksgiving Stuffing. With its outer crunchy texture and soft chewy insides it is truly the most delicious, balanced meal I can think of making for this special evening. It has an extremely buttery, herby flavor that blends well with all the other fixtures expected on a Thanksgiving plate.

Although most people would call this dish "stuffing" as it is commonly used to stuff the turkey before cooking. In my opinion, it is best served as a side item. And lets be honest, Thanksgiving is ALL about the side items! This recipe feeds about 8 people but can be scaled up or down accordingly. If your family is anything like mine, the stuffing is the first to go, so the more the better! I hope your family loves this classic meal as much as mine does!

HAPPY THANKSGIVING!

Ingredients:

  • 12 to 14cups toasted and/or stale bread cubes(1.5 loaves)
  • 1 cup unsalted butter
  • 3 cups diced sweet onion
  • 2 cups diced celery
  • 6 minced garlic cloves
  • salt and pepper
  • 3 tablespooons chopped sage
  • 3 tablespoons chopped parsley
  • 3 tablespoons chopped rosemary
  • 2 1/2 cups chicken stock
  • 2 large eggs
  • a mixture of your favorite herbs for sprinkling

Steps:

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F and coat 9x13 baking dish with butter or oil
  2. Place bread in large mixing bowl or baking dish
  3. Heat butter in large skillet over medium heat. Stirring in onion, celery and garlic
  4. Add salt and pepper and cook until onions and celery soften. About 8-10 minutes
  5. Stir in sage, parsley and rosemary cooking for another minute, Stir in 1 cup of stock
  6. Pour the onion celery mixture over the bread cubess and toss well to coat
  7. In a small bowl, whisk together the remaining 1 1/2 cups of stock and 2 eggs
  8. Pour the mixture onto the bread cubess and stir. Fold bread cubes until combined
  9. Bake the stuffing for 45 to 50 minutes until an internal temperature of 160 F is reached
  10. Note: If the stuffing is turning brown you can tent it with foil
  11. FEAST!
