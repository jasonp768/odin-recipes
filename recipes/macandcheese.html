Baked Macaroni and Cheese

Description:

Everybody loves macaroni and cheese but we all know there is a difference between regular macaroni and cheese and special occasion macaroni and cheese. For thanksgiving, it just seems to hit a little different. It is usually a recipe that has been passed down from generation to generation seemingly getting better with each addition. There truly is no wrong way to create this dish.

To some it is considered the most universal comfort food with many different variations. With its crunchy outer layer and gooey center it creates the perfect mixture for a tasty dish. Any cheese will work but my personal preference is extra-sharp cheddar cheese and colby jack. This is my second favorite dish to cook for any special occasion, especially Thanksgiving. Enjoy!

Ingredients:

  • 8 to 10 oz elbow macaroni
  • 1/2 cup of whole milk
  • 12 oz evaporated milk
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons of black pepper
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons of sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 stick of butter
  • 2 tablespoons of sour cream
  • 1/2 pound of velveeta cut into pieces
  • 8 oz shredded Extra-Sharp Cheddar
  • 8 oz shredded Colby Jack

Steps:

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F
  2. Bring large pot of salted water to a boil
  3. Cook macaroni, al dente and drain
  4. Place cooked macaroni in a 9" by 13" baking dish
  5. Whisk together milk, evaporated milk, eggs, salt, pepper and sugar
  6. Pour mixture over cooked macaroni in baking dish
  7. Add butter, sour cream, velveeta, colby jack and extra-sharp cheese
  8. Combine everything as evenly as possible
  9. Top with shredded cheese of your choice
  10. Bake for 35 to 45 minutes until light brown on top.
  11. Let rest for about 10minutes before serving
  12. Enjoy!
